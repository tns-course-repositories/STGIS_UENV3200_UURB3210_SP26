🧭 Final Project — Stage 1: Ideation & Proposal

🎯 Assignment Overview

This assignment is the first step toward your final project. Your goal is to develop a clear, spatially grounded project idea that builds directly on the concepts and methods introduced throughout the course.

By the end of this stage, you will have: - A focused project theme - A clearly defined spatial question - An initial understanding of the data and methods you will use

This proposal will guide your work in the final weeks of the course.

🧠 Learning Goals

This assignment will help you:

  • Translate real-world interests into spatial questions
  • Connect course methods (vector, raster, remote sensing, spatial analysis methods) to project design
  • Identify appropriate data sources and spatial scales
  • Plan a project that is feasible, clear, and analytically meaningful

🌍 Step 1 — Identify Themes of Interest

List 2–3 broad themes that interest you and align with course topics:

  • Social & physical vulnerability / exposure
  • Demographic patterns & change
  • Climate change & environmental risk
  • Urban systems (mobility, infrastructure, public space)
  • Resource access (food, water, energy)
  • Land use & development pressures
  • Public health & safety (e.g., crashes, pollution)

✅ You may also propose your own theme if it is clearly spatial.

❓ Step 2 — Develop Spatial Questions

For each theme, write 2–3 spatial questions.

Your questions should: - Involve location, distance, pattern, or access - Be answerable using GIS methods from this course - Be clear, even if still exploratory

💡 Use course methods as inspiration:

  • Vector analysis → proximity, buffers, overlays
  • Raster analysis → suitability modeling
  • Remote sensing → land cover / environmental patterns
  • Interpolation → density / hotspot surfaces
  • Network analysis → access, travel distance, service areas

✏️ Example Questions:

  • Where are areas most exposed to both flood risk and low tree canopy?
  • Which neighborhoods lack access to grocery stores within a 10-minute walk?
  • Where are traffic crash hotspots, and who can reach them quickly?
  • How has land cover changed in a rapidly developing area?

📍 Step 3 — Ground Your Idea in Place & Data

Refine your ideas by identifying:

🗺️ Place

  • A specific city, neighborhood, or region
  • Ideally somewhere you are familiar with or interested in

📊 Data

  • Potential spatial datasets (e.g., NYC Open Data, ACS, OpenStreetMap, satellite data)
  • Whether your data is:
    • Vector (points, lines, polygons)
    • Raster (grids, imagery)

📏 Scale

  • Does your question match the scale of your data?
    • Parcel, neighborhood, city, or regional?

👉 Strong projects clearly connect: Place + Data + Method

🧪 Step 4 — Select & Refine Your Project

Choose one theme and one question to move forward with.

Refine it so that it is:

  • ✅ Clearly spatial
  • ✅ Methodologically grounded in course techniques
  • ✅ Feasible within QGIS and course timeframe

🧠 Step 5 — Connect to Course Methods

Identify at least two methods from the course that your project will use:

  • Spatial data models (vector / raster)
  • Thematic mapping & cartography
  • Spatial analysis (proximity, overlay)
  • Raster suitability modeling
  • Remote sensing (earth observation data)
  • Interpolation (continuous surfaces / hotspots)
  • Network analysis (distance, access, service areas)

👉 Briefly explain how these methods will help answer your question.

🧩 Key Reminder

Strong GIS projects are not just about a topic—they are about using the right spatial model to answer a question.

📦 Deliverables

Submit a 1-page proposal (PDF) that includes:

🧭 1. Project Theme

  • Your selected topic area

❓ 2. Spatial Question

  • A clear, concise question

📍 3. Study Area

  • Where your analysis will take place

📊 4. Data Sources (anticipated)

  • List potential datasets

🛠️ 5. Methods

  • At least two course methods you plan to use

✏️ 6. Short Rationale (200–300 words)

  • Why this project matters
  • Why it is spatial
  • What you hope to discover

🧠 Evaluation Criteria

Criteria Weight
Clarity of spatial question 25%
Alignment with course methods 25%
Feasibility (data + scope) 20%
Strength of rationale 20%
Organization & presentation 10%

🚀 Final Note

This proposal is your opportunity to design a project that is:

  • Personally meaningful
  • Spatially rigorous
  • Methodologically grounded

Take time to explore ideas—but aim to leave this assignment with a clear and actionable direction.
